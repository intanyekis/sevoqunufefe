Stranger Things Star Noah Schnapp Is Making Waves With His Healthy Nutella Alternative

There’s a healthier version of Nutella on the market, and we have Stranger Things star Noah Schnapp to thank for the new hazelnut spread.

Partnering with Umana Venture Studios, the actor has launched his brand TBH (To Be Honest). Inspired by his vegan lifestyle, Noah wanted to create a hazelnut spread that’s vegan and climate conscious, hence the delicious product.

Noah previously he was motivated to create TBH after he learned how damaging Nutella (and other similar spreads) are for the environment. Palm oil is central to Nutella, but it’s one of the main causes of deforestation and habitat damage.

In addition to being vegan, TBH is gluten-free and kosher. It also contains 6g of protein. It also claims to have 50% less sugar than its competitors.

Although Noah launched the vegan treat last year, but it’s been making headlines again after Buzzfeed released a taste-test article to see if the hype was worth it. The article said that TBH is runnier, not as sweet, and has a stronger hazelnut flavor than Nutella, which is more cocoa-focused.

One thing to note, however, is that Nutella is more accessible than TBH. Buzzfeed notes that Nutella averages for $3.67/13 ounces in store, while TBH is $11.30/14 ounces, making it triple the price.

While sales figures aren’t available for TBH, it’s likely to help expand Noah’s net worth, so long as it continues with its popularity.
