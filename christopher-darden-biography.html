Christopher Darden Bio (Wiki)

Who is Christopher Darden?

Christopher Darden is an American Lawyer apart from that he is also known as an author, actor, lecturer, and practicing attorney.

In 1994 he was recruited in the famous O.J Simpson murder trial as a second command for the prosecution along with leading prosecutor Marcia Clark

Christopher Darden: Age, Parents, Ethnicity, Education

He was born on April 7, 1956, in Richmond, California, He is the son of Richard and Jean. His father has worked as a shipyard welder, and his mother is a school cafeteria employee.

He completed his B.S. in Criminal Justice Administration from San Jose State University in 1977 letter he received a Juris Doctor from the University of California, Hastings College of Law in 1980.

Christopher Darden is an American citizen but his ethnicity is not disclosed.

Christopher Darden: Career, Salary, and Net Worth

Christopher Darden started his career on the national labor relations board in Los Angeles as a  lawyer later he was assigned to the Huntington Park office and finally to the Criminal Courts Building (CCB) in downtown Los Angeles in late 1983.

After resigning from the O.J. Simpson case he joined the faculty at California state university, Los Angeles. Furthermore, he taught and specialized in criminal procedure and trial advocacy at Southwestern University School of law as an associate producer.

Besides that, he is also a former legal commentator on various TV channels like CNN, Court TV, NBC, and CNBC.

He has appeared as a guest on TV series and talk shows like Touched by an Angel, Girlfriends, The Tonight Show with Jay Leno, The Howard Stern Show, Muppets Tonight, Roseanne, and the movie Liar Liar alongside Actor Jim Carry.

Christopher Darden has also worked in the syndicated legal show Power of Attorney as a principal attorney.

His talent does not limit to the field of advocacy and acting only he is also an author. He is the author of the best-selling crime novel  “In Contempt” (1996).

Later he co-author many crime novels including  “The Trials of Nikki Hill” (1999) and “LA Justice” (2000) and “The Last Defense,(2002), with Dick Lochte.

For awards and recognition, he won the Crystal Heart Award from Loved Ones of Homicide Victims in 1998, and in 2002 he was named “Humanitarian of the Year” by Eli Horne, a California shelter for abused women and children.

Christopher Darden has an estimated net worth of $2 million and his salary is unknown as of 2022.

Is Christopher Darden involved in any rumors and controversies?

There were rumors that Darden was romancing a lead prosecutor during O.J Simpson’s murder case but later both dismissed the rumors saying that they were trench mates and best of friends.

During the case, Christopher Darden asked the murderer to fit bloodied gloves that were big enough to fit. This case was defended by the star defense attorney, Johnnie Cochran, best known for his now-famous quip, “If it doesn’t fit, you must acquit.”

Further, this case was hyped as a racial case rather than a murder case by the media between two black lawyers.
