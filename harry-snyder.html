Career

Together with his wife Esther Snyder, he founded In-N-Out Burger. Snyder was one of the first people to develop the idea of a hamburger restaurant, where customers could order their food via an intercom. His wife and he opened the first In-Out-Burger on October 22, 1948, in the Los Angeles suburbs of Baldwin Park, California. In-N-Out is described as California’s first drive. By the mid-1950s, the chain had grown to six stores in the Los Angeles area. By the time Snyder died of lung cancer in 1976, the company had grown to 18 stores.
